Mission

Cal State LA Special Collections & Archives supports the discovery, learning and engagement goals of the University by identifying, acquiring, preserving, and providing access to primary research materials of enduring value in support of the academic mission of the university, the educational and research needs of the Cal State LA academic community, scholars, and the research community at large. As a repository situated in East Los Angeles, and at the heart of several diasporic, immigrant communities, Special Collections & Archives moreover aims to capture the diverse lives and historical narratives reflected in our heavily first-generation student body, and the major contributions their communities have made to the dynamic cultures of Southern California.

Technical Skill Snapshot

Azalea came into SCA without very many technical skills and had to learn them on the job because when she entered the archive, there was no infrastructure set up for the technical aspects. She created the metadata standards for the collections and learned about customized and local schemas for the community-based collections. Do entry level archivists need to have these skills right away? Azalea recommended for us to become comfortable with digital repositories and metadata before entering into the field. Another skill she highlighted as important to know is how digital repositories intersect with other content management and discovery systems. Cal State LA uses Alma/Primo, which is the discovery system for the University Library. The skills you need depend on the job you’re applying for. SCA was looking for a digital preservationist and metadata experts to fill a gap in the current team. She thinks tech will come and go and that AI needs to be thought of more critically (use, how to respond, emotional/intellectual impact). doesn’t feel as though AI can remove the people/community aspect from librarianship and archivism and that the process itself connects the human experience. though AI does exacerbate the loneliness aspect of the digital sphere. emphasizes that the soft skills are incredibly important for archival/librarian careers, that people skills and communication are paramount above tech skills (which can always be learned).
