Assingment Instructions

Tasks

  • Form a group (week 1-2)
  • Identify 1 IS professional off campus. They should both have different profiles. It is okay if they do multiple things in their role.
  • Do secondary research, prepare a questionnaire for an interview - cover all the requirements of the assignment.
  • Conduct an interview with the professional over zoom or in-person and record the interview (if they permit). Ask them about the ecological landscape of their work (technical tools and infrastructures, social and collaborative practices, institutional and political constraints, invisible labor, maintenance, or care work, skills required to succeed and adapt and finally, how AI is impacting the future of their work).You can upload the interview (audio only or video as well) to your GitHub site eventually. If not possible, you should upload a redacted transcript to the site.
  • Synthesize insights from the interview, analyze and prepare a specialization dossier. This dossier will be presented in class and shared with your classmates - each dossier will serve as a knowledge artefact to support you and your fellow LIS practitioners in navigating your professional practice.
    • This dossier should contain all the information resources that your audience (classmates) can review for their own professional success. This dossier will be based on your interviews as well as any resources that your specialist points you to *and* secondary research by the group (find mailing lists, websites, tutorials, Slack or Discord channels...). The dossier should also contain a summary of individual contributions to the project. Publish the dossier on a live and public GitHub website.
  • Conduct a class presentation (12 minutes) in Week 10. The entire group must participate (if you are going to be absent in class due to exceptional circumstances, you must get the professor's approval to present on zoom and the dossier should list your contributions). It should be based on the dossier but can also include what you learned in the process and how it has shaped your thinking about your professional future (every group member must speak).

Interview questions and the final dossier should address the following:

  • Overview: 300 words (or, for your presentation, a few sentences) that summarize the main points you think your fellow students should be aware of.
  • Typical tasks: What does a job in this field entail? What duties does someone with this specialization perform? Provide links to at least three job descriptions (either current or out of date is fine) at different levels of seniority.
  • Professional context: Is there a professional association for this specialization? What conferences do people in this specialization attend? What journals publish in this area? Where are jobs posted? Are there other forums, such as blogs, listservs, or Discord sites, that your classmates should be aware of?
  • Job-market outlook: Is this field growing? Shrinking? Are parts of it expanding while other parts contract? This will necessarily be impressionistic, rather than statistical, since you likely won’t have access to numbers. To gain a sense of this, you should read relevant professional literature (see 3, above) and check in with your expert.
  • Technical Skill snapshot: What collection of skills is expected in someone hired in this specialization at the entry level? Do you predict a growing demand for particular skills? In order to judge this, you’ll need to look at job ads, read professional literature, and speak with your expert.
  • Training agenda: In order to enter this profession, what kinds of preparation should someone at your professional level undertake? Are there particular classes they should take? Should they be looking for particular kinds of internships? Should they target particular technical skills?
  • Labor trends: Are there particular tendencies within the field (e.g., unpaid internships, very low pay, temporary contracts, a history of discrimination) that your classmates should be on the lookout for? If so, how are professionals in this area agitating for change (e.g., union drives, advocacy campaigns, etc.)? What kind of structural change (e.g., an end to temporary contracts) would have the best chance of addressing these trends?
  • Five ways to understand the profession. An annotated bibliography of the five most helpful resources you encountered in your research. These can be articles, books, a blog post, a podcast, a video: whatever you think will be most helpful to your fellow students. Each resource should be accompanied by a few sentences that describe the resource and explain why it’s helpful.
