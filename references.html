Annoted Bibliography

“Resources.” RBMS, May 24, 2021. https://rbms.info/resources/.

The resources section of the Rare Books and Manuscript Section of the Association of College and Research Libraries puts job listings, publications, educational opportunities, and other aspects and necessities of the field in one convenient place.

Altman, Burt. “A Day in the Life of a Special Collections Archivist.” Illuminations, December 16, 2020. https://fsuspecialcollections.wordpress.com/2012/10/11/a-day-in-the-life-of-a-special-collections-archivist/.

This is an article written by Florida State University special collections librarian Burt Altman, who works there as an archivist and archival manager. The post describes what a typical work day in a special collections library looks like and explains some of what to expect in the role.

“Activity: Learn About Special Collections.” Graduate Student Research Institute.

https://sites.uw.edu/libid/services-and-resources/activity-libraries-services-resources/activity-learn-about-special-collections/.

This webpage from the University of Washington breaks down (common) areas and subjects of special collections as well as explains finding aids and how they work.

“Guides: Special Collections & University Archives Best Practices Manual: Processing.” Guides at Johns Hopkins University. https://guides.library.jhu.edu/c.php?g=1242855&p=9302622#:~:text=%22Archival%20processing%E2%80%9D%20refers%20to%20the,used%20in%20a%20finding%20aid.

This guide from John Hopkins University gives an overview of archival processing and the principles behind them such as provenance, respect des fonds, and original order.

“Special Collections: What We Collect.” CMU Libraries. https://www.library.cmu.edu/policies/collection-development-special-collections.

Carnegie Mellon University Library provides an outline of what their special collection collects and the criteria they use when accepting new materials. This outline can be used as a tool for understanding how an item qualifies to be in a special collection and the process it must go through to get there.
